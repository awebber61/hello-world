Poetry

Lost in Prayer

Lately I’ve been calling on the Saint of Lost Causes.
Living through touch, I’ve been kissing his feet,
And letting my salt burn into the floor.
But long pauses still fill my prayers.

I’ve been slowly drowning,
For forty days, My body
Wracked numb from pain.
And the black noise, it trembles.

I long for the blows of daily life,
To strike the match, and let it burn.
To return me to that rocking feeling,
That never-ending wave,
That carries me back home.

I’ve been trying to remember how the sun sets;
How the sky sparks into flames.
The clouds becoming the tinder,
That rest upon the spinning earth,
Where sunset never ends.

Lost in a tidal wave, I seek refuge.
I pray to a God. But,
Why need God, when there is good?
Can’t I see, the extra letter,
Makes the invisible, tangible.

When I am starved of hope,
My body turned cold,
And my tank on empty.
How the untended fire
Still burns, I do not know.



– Annie Webber


Thus passes the glory of the world

Let’s just sit here, feel lousy
And watch the grass grow.
Let’s stop the grinding sound
Our bones create when we move.

Fetch me that dusty checkered blanket,
Let’s eat buttered toast with jam jars of scotch.
Or let’s run through the meadow, laughing,
Till its set aflame by the setting sun.

Let’s return to the warmth,
With a dance around the stove,
To The Mamas and the Papas, drumming
On skillets with slotted spoons.

Let’s avoid the time before sleep,
When daily distractions flicker out
like fireflies fleeing fast
from lights touch.
Let’s stop the lion’s roar,
That shreds our stomachs.​
And the oil spill of regret, that slowly
Poisons the little creature inside.

Let’s dream of someone else,
Who’s shape the bed truly holds,
But, for now, we can cling
To each other, to just breathe.


– Annie Webber



The Sun Rising

After you mix two souls,
Combine the brightest blues
With the deepest reds
It becomes heavy.

When you get up and leave,
Can you tell which bits are yours,
And which are theirs?
Is it possible to be untangled, or

Do you leave holding pieces that don't match?
Left with gaps that feel hollow,
Can you get them back?
Can you grow anew?
It seems as though,
When you walk away from love,
You’ll always be carrying too little,
Or a little too much.

- John Donne
